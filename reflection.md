# Profile Comparison Reflections

**High-Energy Pop vs. Chill Lofi.** These two profiles sit at opposite ends of almost every axis — opposite energy, tempo, and acousticness targets — and their top-5 lists share zero songs. High-Energy Pop surfaces fast, danceable tracks like *Sunrise City* and *Rooftop Lights*, while Chill Lofi returns slow, textured tracks like *Midnight Coding* and *Library Rain*. This is the clearest case where numeric proximity does exactly what you'd expect.

**Deep Intense Rock vs. High Energy + Sad [ADVERSARIAL].** Both profiles target high energy (0.90), but Deep Intense Rock pairs it with a consistent "intense rock" mood and genre that exists in the catalog, while High Energy + Sad asks for sad folk — a contradiction, since sad folk songs are typically low-energy. Deep Intense Rock's top score reaches 1.22; the sad profile's top score is only 0.87, and its #1 song (*Porch Song*) got there mostly through its +0.30 genre/mood bonus despite having an audio score of just 0.57. When mood and energy conflict, the system can't satisfy both signals and the bonus system takes over.

**High-Energy Pop vs. Ghost Genre & Mood [ADVERSARIAL].** High-Energy Pop's genre ("pop") and mood ("happy") both exist in the catalog, so the +0.15 bonuses fire regularly and its top score reaches 1.24. Ghost Genre & Mood uses "reggae" and "euphoric" — neither is in the catalog — so no bonuses ever fire and the top score is capped at 0.92, limited purely by how close any song can get numerically. This pair shows how much the categorical bonuses influence the ranking: a slightly weaker audio match can still win simply by sharing a genre or mood label.

**Dead Center vs. Ghost Genre & Mood [ADVERSARIAL].** Both profiles produce compressed numeric scores across the catalog, but for different reasons. Ghost Genre & Mood's ranking is honest — the top song (0.92) genuinely has the best audio match. Dead Center sets every numeric target to 0.5, making all songs nearly equidistant, but its valid pop/happy preferences still trigger bonuses. *Sunrise City* has only a 0.70 audio score but wins at 1.00 total, while *Midnight Coding* (0.88 audio score) places third because it earns no bonus. When numeric scores flatten, the bonus system quietly takes over the ranking.

**Impossible Tempo vs. Deep Intense Rock.** Both profiles want high energy and similar valence, but Impossible Tempo sets its tempo target to 300 BPM — beyond the scorer's 200-BPM ceiling — making every song's tempo proximity essentially the same near-zero value. With tempo contributing nothing, the other features and categorical bonuses absorb all the discriminative weight. *Current State* wins for Impossible Tempo not because it sounds right, but because it earns the electronic/energetic genre and mood bonus (+0.30). Deep Intense Rock, with a realistic 145 BPM target, produces a more coherent ranking where *Storm Runner* wins on genuine audio proximity plus bonuses.

**Chill Lofi vs. Contradictory Acoustic [ADVERSARIAL].** Both profiles set `likes_acoustic=True`, but Chill Lofi also sets `target_acousticness=0.72`, while Contradictory Acoustic sets `target_acousticness=0.00`. Since the scoring formula uses only the numeric target and ignores the boolean flag entirely, the Contradictory Acoustic profile is scored as if the user wants fully electronic sound. Chill Lofi recommends acoustic-textured tracks; Contradictory Acoustic recommends low-acousticness tracks like *Night Drive Loop* and *Storm Runner*. The `likes_acoustic` boolean is a silent no-op — a real user checking that preference would have no idea it changed nothing.
